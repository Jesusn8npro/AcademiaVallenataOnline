Retarget that BVH walk onto my character's rig. Method that works for CloudRig:
1) Import the BVH with fps-rescaling to match the scene. The imported frame 1 is usually a junk rest pose (hips at 0), start on frame 2.
2) UPPER BODY = FK orientation transfer. Use the character's 'Properties' bone: ik_*_arm/leg = 0, Spine_ik = 0, strictly use FK control. For each bone, force its ABSOLUTE world orientation from the source using delta rotation_euler.
Map: Hips -> TORSO-Spine (also its world pos), Spine -> Spine1/2/3, Neck/Head -> FK-Neck/Head, shoulders/arms -> FK-Shoulder/UpperArm/Forearm/Wrist, toes -> Toes. Scale = target_leg_length / source_leg_length.
3) LEGS = IK by POSITION, not orientation. ⚠️ Orientation-driving the thighs amplifies sideways adduction (roll-axis mismatch) and CROSSES the legs even though the source walk is uncrossed. Instead: switch legs to IK and drive each IK-Foot by position: target = RainPelvis_world + (srcFoot_world - srcHips_world) * Scale.
This guarantees the correct side AND auto-locks the planted foot (no skating).
4) GROUND IT: shift TORSO-Spine AND both IK-Foot Z keys together so the lowest foot sole vertex sits at z = 0. Use a clamped vertical "bob" so an outlier frame can't sink the body.
5) Widen the stance a few cm per side if the feet track too close to the centerline.
6) When done, DELETE the giant source rig. It's ~30x the character and will look like a "massive rig" baked onto the rig.
Verify as you go by rendering the viewport from front, behind, and 3/4 with a temporary ground plane, and show me the feet crossing/sliding/floating before calling it done.
